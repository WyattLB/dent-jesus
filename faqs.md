# Dent Jesus - Paintless Dent Repair FAQs

## 1. What is paintless dent repair (PDR)?
Paintless dent repair is a process of removing dents from your vehicle's metal panels without using body filler or repainting. Technicians gently massage the metal back to its original shape while keeping your factory finish intact.

## 2. What types of dents can you fix?
We can repair most small to medium dents, door dings, and hail damage as long as the paint is not cracked and the metal is not excessively stretched. A quick in-person or photo estimate confirms if PDR is right.

## 3. How long does a typical PDR repair take?
Many small dents and door dings can be fixed in a few hours. Larger dents or heavy hail damage may take a full day or more, depending on the number of dents, panel access, and your insurance process.

## 4. How much does paintless dent repair cost?
Pricing depends on dent size, depth, location, and how many panels are affected. Small door dings are usually inexpensive, while complex or hail repairs cost more. We provide a clear estimate before any work is started.

## 5. Will my dent be completely gone?
In most eligible cases, the dent is restored so it is virtually undetectable. Extremely deep or stretched metal may show a minor flaw under certain light, but the overall appearance and value improve significantly.

## 6. Will PDR damage my paint?
No. PDR is specifically designed to preserve your factory paint. We work behind the panel or use glue pulling on the outside without sanding or repainting, so your original finish and warranty stay intact.

## 7. Can you fix hail damage?
Yes. Hail repair is one of the best uses of PDR. We remove dozens or even hundreds of small dents across the vehicle, often working with your insurance company to restore your car without traditional bodywork.

## 8. Do you work with insurance companies?
Yes. We regularly handle insurance claims for hail and other comprehensive damage. We can help with the estimate, communicate with your adjuster, and make the repair process as simple and stress free as possible.

## 9. Will a PDR repair show up on my vehicle history report?
Typically, paintless dent repair does not appear on Carfax or other vehicle history reports because no parts are replaced and no repainting is reported. This helps you maintain your vehicle's resale value.

## 10. Can you repair aluminum panels?
Many aluminum panels can be repaired with PDR, but the process can be more time consuming and technically demanding. We will inspect the damage first and let you know if aluminum PDR is a good option.

## 11. What if the paint is cracked or chipped?
If the paint is cracked, traditional body repair or touch-up may be needed along with or instead of PDR. We will explain your options, including any blended solutions that balance appearance, cost, and value.

## 12. Is PDR better than traditional body shop repair?
For eligible dents, PDR is usually faster, more affordable, and preserves your factory paint. Traditional bodywork is still the best choice when damage is severe, paint is missing, or the metal is badly distorted.

## 13. Can PDR fix dents on body lines or edges?
Many body-line dents can be repaired if the paint is intact and the metal is not overstretched. Panel edges are more limited. Our technician will evaluate these areas carefully and set realistic expectations.

## 14. Do I need an appointment for an estimate?
Appointments are recommended so we can give you dedicated time, but we may also accept walk-ins when the schedule allows. You can often text or email photos first for a quick preliminary estimate.

## 15. Will PDR affect my factory warranty?
Because PDR does not disturb your factory paint or structural components, it should not affect your paint or corrosion warranty. We simply return the metal to its original shape without altering the finish.

## 16. Can you fix lease return or pre-sale dents?
Yes. PDR is ideal for cleaning up lease returns and vehicles you plan to sell. Removing visible dents helps you avoid lease penalties and makes your car more attractive to buyers or dealers.

## 17. What areas of my vehicle can you repair?
We typically repair metal body panels like hoods, roofs, doors, fenders, and quarter panels. Plastic bumpers and trim usually cannot be fixed with PDR, but we can advise on the best repair options.

## 18. Do you offer any warranty on your repairs?
Most PDR repairs come with a limited lifetime warranty against the dent returning on the repaired area for as long as you own the vehicle. We will explain the exact warranty terms with your estimate.

## 19. Can I use a DIY kit instead of professional PDR?
DIY kits can easily stretch metal or crack paint if used incorrectly, making professional repair more difficult and expensive. Having a trained technician perform PDR is the safest way to protect your vehicle.
